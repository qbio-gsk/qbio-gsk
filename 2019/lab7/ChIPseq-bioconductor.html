Functional genomics assays for transcriptional regulation and epigenetics

Transcriptional regulation and epigenetics

  • Cells with identical or nearly identical genomes in the organism have very different phenotypes and gene expression profiles

  • Could be explained by epigenetic differences:
    • differences in transcription factor and other protein binding to chromatin
    • DNA modifications (methylation)
    • histone modifications
    • chromatin accessibility
    • chromatin interactions in 3D

  • Sequencing-based assays to study transcriptional regulation and epigenetics

Protein binding or histone modifications

  • ChIP-seq; also: ChIP-nexus, ChIP-exo, Cut&Run

Chromatin accessibility

  • ATAC-seq; also: DNase-seq, MNase-seq, FAIRE-seq

Chromatin interactions

  • Hi-C; also: HiChIP, ChIA-PET

Sequencing-based assays

Genomic intervals and annotations

File formats

  • BED
    • Browser Extensible Data
    • tab-delimited
    • required fields: chrom, chromStart, chromEnd
    • optional fields: name, score, strand, etc.
  • GFF/GTF
    • General Feature Format / General Transfer Format
    • tab-delimited
    • required fields: seqname, source, feature, start, end, score, strand, frame, attribute
